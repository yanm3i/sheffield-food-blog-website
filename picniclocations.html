Picnic Locations

This page highlights some of the best picnic spots in Sheffield!

Western Park

Western Park on Google Maps
Source

Found next to the Arts Tower, Western Park is a wonderful picnic spot on a nice and sunny day to settle down near the duck pond and admire the forestry!

Fun Fact: Western Park was designed by Robert Marnock, a designer who also contributed to other major parks in Sheffield such as the Botanical Gardens!

Crookes Valley Park

Crookes Valley on Google Maps
Source

Close to the Goodwin Sports Hall, Crookes Valley Park is a beautiful location to take in the beauty of nature. With a playground for children, various benches, courts for sports and a large lake, it's the perfect spot to settle down and enjoy the presence of nature.

Fun Fact: Found in the park is the Dam House cafe & restaurant for when you're feeling a bit peckish!

South Street Park

South Street Park on Google Maps

If you're looking for a wide spacious area outside of the University campus, South Street Park is the perfect open space to view the city of Sheffield. Found near Sheffield Station, it can easily be reached through a short tram trip and an adventurous trek to gain the full experience of witnessing magnificient views & the perfect picnic place during the sunniest of days!

Fun Fact: Due to the wide open space, South Street Park tends to host public amphithreatre events where an audience can easily watch performances from multiple sides of the hill!

The Ponderosa

Ponderosa on Google Maps
Source

The Ponderosa is a large open space that has been used to host community events and sports events. With it's large meadow, welcome to various types of wildlife, it's a lovely location to have a picnic and enjoy the extended open space.

Fun Fact: The Ponderosa was actually given it's name by the local children in the 1960s, named after a ranch in the popular 60s TV series, Bonanza!
